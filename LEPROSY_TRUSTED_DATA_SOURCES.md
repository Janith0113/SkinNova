## 🗂️ Tier 5: Specific Training Data Categories

### Category 1: Disease Classification
**Source**: WHO, ILA
```
Type: Medical Information
Key Points:
- Indeterminate Leprosy (IL): Early, often self-healing
- Tuberculoid Leprosy (TT): Localized, strong immunity, few lesions
- Borderline Tuberculoid (BT): Similar to TT
- Mid-Borderline (BB): Unstable, intermediate
- Borderline Lepromatous (BL): Many bacilli, impaired immunity
- Lepromatous Leprosy (LL): Disseminated, high bacilli, weak immunity

Clinical Manifestation Guide:
- Lesion distribution
- Number of lesions
- Sensory loss patterns
- Nerve involvement extent
- Bacteriological index
```

### Category 2: Treatment Protocols

**Source**: CDC, WHO
```
Paucibacillary (PB) - 6 months:
- Rifampicin 600mg/month + 
- Ofloxacin 400mg/day +
- Minocycline 100mg/day

Multibacillary (MB) - 12 months:
- Rifampicin 600mg/month +
- Ofloxacin 400mg/day +
- Minocycline 100mg/day +
- Ofloxacin 400mg/month

Monitoring Points:
- Monthly clinical assessment
- Slit-skin smear at 0, 6, 12 months
- Adverse effect monitoring
- Clinically-effective outcome: 6 months
- Bacteriologically-effective outcome: 12 months
```

### Category 3: Leprosy Reactions

**Source**: CDC, ILA
```
Type 1 Reaction (Reversal Reaction):
- Cell-mediated immunity dysregulation
- Timing: During or within 1 year after treatment
- Signs: Inflammation of lesions, neuritis, edema
- Treatment: Corticosteroids (prednisolone)

Type 2 Reaction (Érythème Nodosum Leprosum):
- Immune complex deposition
- Timing: During treatment, more in BL & LL
- Signs: Red nodules, fever, iridocyclitis, neuritis
- Treatment: Corticosteroids, Thalidomide, or both
```

### Category 4: Disability Prevention

**Source**: WHO, Leprosy Mission
```
Grade 0 (No disability): No signs of disability in eyes, hands, feet
Grade 1 (Some disability): Loss of sensation or visible deformity
Grade 2 (Severe disability): Visible deformity or lagophthalmos with ulceration

Prevention Measures:
1. Early detection (within 1 year of onset)
2. Appropriate MDT
3. Early recognition of reactions
4. Proper foot care (Grade 2 protection)
5. Psychological support
6. Rehabilitation training
```

### Category 5: Nutrition & Lifestyle

**Source**: WHO, ILA, Nutritional Research
```
Dietary Recommendations:
- Protein: 1.5-2g/kg daily (tissue repair)
- Vitamins: A, C, E, B complex (immune support)
- Minerals: Zinc, selenium, copper
- Iron: For anemia prevention
- Hydration: 2-3L water daily

Lifestyle Modifications:
- Sleep: 7-9 hours nightly
- Stress management: Meditation, counseling
- Exercise: 30 min, 5x/week moderate activity
- Avoid: Smoking, excessive alcohol
- UV protection: Essential (photosensitivity)
```
